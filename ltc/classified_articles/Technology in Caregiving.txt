This document outlines guidelines for conducting foot care in home nursing settings. The key components are:

1. **Foot Health Assessment**: Includes detailed examination of toes (including nail condition), feet, skin health, blood supply evaluation at the toe web spaces, and asking about diabetes history, medication use, and blood sugar control.

2. **Correct Nail Trimming Technique**: Emphasizes trimming nails straight across with rounded edges to prevent ingrown toenails. 

3. **Foot Bathing Procedures**:
   - Temperature: 41-43°C for general soaking; cooler (35-40°C) if there are wounds.
   - Duration: 10-15 minutes, shorter duration if there are open sores or injuries.

4. **Scrubbing Tools**: Use of a loofah sponge or wet towel to gently remove hard skin and calluses on heels, toes, and nail edges.

5. **Post-Care Grooming**: Includes addressing wounds, managing ingrown toenails, and cleaning undernail debris with specialized tools like an orange stick or cuticle scissors. 

6. **Disinfection Protocol**:
   - Clean instruments after each use.
   - Use chlorine water to disinfect tools.
   - Store sanitized items in a clean container.

7. **Care Instructions for Family/Caregivers**: Emphasizes the importance of regular foot care, correct nail trimming methods, and daily moisturizing routines. 

8. **Follow-Up Care Evaluation**:
   - Assess patient's understanding of foot problems and prevention measures.
   - Evaluate caregiver's ability to perform foot care independently.

9. **Referral Guidelines**: Suggests professional medical referral for individuals with diabetes, hypertension, ulcers, or wounds.

10. **Documentation**: Requires comprehensive records including assessment forms, teaching sheets, follow-up evaluations, and adherence to long-term care service laws or relevant healthcare regulations.

This guideline serves as a reference tool for healthcare providers delivering foot care services in home settings, emphasizing proper technique, patient education, and infection control measures.
